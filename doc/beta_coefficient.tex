Current diffuse and reflect input data of InExEs are taken from a horizontal device. Considering the diffuse source (reflect one is the same) as uniform on a hemisphere, for a horizontal plane we need to normalize this data with the integral of Lambert's law over the hemisphere itself. So,

\begin{equation}
I_{dif}^{inp} = \int_{0}^{2 \pi} \int_{0}^{\pi / 2} I_{dif} \cos \theta \sin \theta d \theta d \phi = \pi I_{dif} 
\end{equation}

Irradiance for every point over a hemisphere is so

\begin{equation}
I_{dif} = \frac{I_{dif}^{inp}}{\pi}
\end{equation}

The total amount of irradiance received from a surface with an angle $\gamma$ between its normal and the vector perpendicular with the horizon is

\begin{equation}
I_{dif}^{rec} = \alpha( \gamma )  \int_{A_f} I_{dif} \cos \theta d \Omega
\end{equation}

Where 

\begin{equation}
\alpha = \frac{1 + \cos \gamma}{2}
\end{equation}

takes into account the ratio of visible upper hemisphere and $A_f$ is the total free solid angle not covered from other surfaces. Solving the integral

\begin{equation}
I_{dif}^{rec} = I_{dif}^{inp} (1 + \cos \gamma) \int_{x \in f(A_f)} x d x
\end{equation}
